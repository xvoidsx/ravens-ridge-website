Miso Ramen

A "comfort hug" in a bowl with a rich, nutty broth.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups chicken/vegetable stock
  • 3 garlic cloves (smashed)
  • 2-inch piece of ginger (sliced, this is for the stock)
  • 1 tsp ginger(grated)
  • 2 dried shiitake mushrooms (I also use fresh)
  • 3 tbsp miso paste
  • 1 tbsp tahini (or peanut butter)
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 2 blocks of Ramen noodles (I use Simply Asia because it's all that's available in my area, you can use fresh if you have them)

    • Instructions

    1. Simmer stock with smashed garlic, sliced ginger, and shiitake mushrooms for 15 minutes. This makes a store-bought stock taste like it was simmered all day.
    2. Cook your ramen noodles (fresh or dried) in a separate pot. Drain them well.
    3. Whisk miso paste, tahini (or peanut butter), honey, and grated ginger in a small bowl. This is the miso tare.
    4. Place the miso tare in the bottom of your serving bowls. Pour a little bit of the hot broth over it and whisk until smooth, then fill the rest of the bowl with broth.
    5. Slide the noodles into the broth and add toppings. Top with a pat of butter for enhanced flavor.

    Recipe Notes

    • By adding the miso tare to the bowls and then mixing in the broth slowly, you preserve the probiotics of the miso.
    • I like to add the jammy(soft boiled) egg. Just boil the egg for 6-6.5 minutes
    • Sweet corn and scallions are typical toppings but I also like to used blanched baby bok choy. Sometimes I will add kimchi for an extra flavor boost
