Honey-Soy Eggplant

Ingredients

  • 2 long Japanese eggplants or 1 medium globe eggplant
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar

Instructions

  1. Slice 2 long Japanese eggplants into 1-inch rounds. If using the globe eggplant, it works, but it has more moisture and seeds. Peel some of the thick skin off in "stripes" (like a zebra), then cube it. Lightly salt the cubes for 10 minutes and pat them dry before frying to keep them from getting mushy.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and fry the eggplant rounds until both sides are golden brown and the centers are creamy (about 3–4 mins per side). On the globe eggplant, fry until golden brown, turning occasionally to avoid burning.
  3. Whisk soy sauce, honey, and rice vinegar together for sauce.
  4. Pour the glaze into the hot pan. It will bubble and thicken instantly. Toss the eggplant to coat every piece until it looks like "purple jewels."

Recipe Notes

  • I have found that even after salting the globe eggplant that it stays softer when cooked (which I don't mind). I also don't use as much salt as probably necessary to get rid of more moisture.
  • Because I am more conscious of sodium intake, I will sometimes reduce the soy sauce to 1 tbsp.
  • I pair this with jasmine rice and use it as a side dish.
