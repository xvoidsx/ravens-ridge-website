Salmon Miso-ni

A soul-warming, savory dish

Ingredients

  • 2 large Salmon fillets (or Mackerel/Cod), skin removed
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2-inch ginger piece (sliced into coins)
  • 3 tbsp Miso paste (Red or Awase)
  • Shredded ginger (needle-cut)
  • scallions

    • Instructions

    1. Cut your skinless fillets into large, bite-sized chunks. To ensure the "cleanest" flavor, briefly blanch the fish in boiling water for 30 seconds, then drain and rinse.
    2. In a wide pan, combine the water, sake, mirin, sugar, and ginger slices. Bring to a simmer.
    3. Carefully place the fish in the pan. Cover with a "drop lid" (or a piece of parchment paper cut to fit inside the pan) and simmer on medium-low for 10 minutes.
    4. Take a ladle of the hot cooking liquid and whisk it into the miso paste in a small bowl until smooth. Pour this back into the pan.
    5. Spoon the sauce over the fish and simmer uncovered for another 5 minutes until the sauce thickens into a glossy glaze.
    6. Plate the fish with plenty of the ginger-miso sauce and top with the fresh needle-ginger.

    Recipe Notes

    • In a pinch, I substitute cooking sherry for sake. It's too expensive here for me to cook with it regularly.
    • I typically serve this with jasmine rice and steamed veggies.
