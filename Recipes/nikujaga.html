Nikujaga

A sweet and savory stew that defines "home cooking".

Ingredients

  • 3/4 lb chicken breast, cut into bite-sized chunks or 1/2 lb thinly sliced ribeye or chuck (paper-thin "bulgogi" style)
  • 3 medium potatoes (Yukon Gold or Russet)
  • 1 large carrot
  • 1 yellow onion
  • 1.5 cups dashi or broth of choice
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • Boiled snap peas or scallions

    • Instructions

    1. Peel and cut potatoes and carrots into "rolling wedges" (Ran-giri style) for more surface area. Slice the onion into thick wedges.
    2. In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat a little oil. Sauté the chicken/beef and onions until the onions are translucent.
    3. Add the potatoes and carrots. Pour in the dashi, sake, mirin, and honey.
    4. Bring to a boil, skim off any foam. Reduce heat to medium-low. Place a "drop lid" (parchment paper) on top and simmer for 15-20 mins until potatoes are tender.
    5. Add the soy sauce last (this keeps the color bright). Simmer for 5 more minutes.
    6. Let the stew sit for at least 10 minutes before serving; the flavors deepen as it cools slightly.

    Recipe Notes

    • In a pinch, I substitute cooking sherry for sake. It's too expensive here for me to cook with it regularly.
    • You can blanch the beef in boiling water for 10 seconds to remove excess fat and make a cleaner broth.
    • I prefer gold potatoes for both the chicken and beef versions but I have read that Russet potatoes absorb the flavor of beef better.
