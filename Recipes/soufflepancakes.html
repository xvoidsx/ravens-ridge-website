Cloud-Soft Soufflé Pancakes

Airy, pillowy pancakes served with a bright honey-citrus compote.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs (separated)
  • 1.5 tbsp whole milk
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup cake flour (sifted)
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp lemon or yuzu juice
  • fresh berries to serve

    • Instructions

    1. Whisk egg yolks, milk, and vanilla until thick. Sift in flour and baking powder; whisk until just combined.
    2. In a separate clean bowl, beat egg whites until frothy. Gradually add sugar, beating until stiff, glossy peaks form.
    3. Gently fold 1/3 of the meringue into the yolk mixture to lighten it. Add the rest and fold with a spatula with extreme care—you want to keep the air bubbles!
    4. Lightly oil a non-stick pan over very low heat. Scoop tall mounds of batter onto the pan. Add 1 tsp of water to empty spaces and cover with a lid to steam.
    5. After 5–6 mins, when the bottom is golden, very carefully flip. Add another tsp of water, cover, and cook for 5 more mins.
    6. Top with the honey-citrus compote and berries immediately before they deflate.

    Recipe Notes

    • Sometimes I am impatient and cook these as normal pancakes without the meringue or steaming. They still taste just as good.
