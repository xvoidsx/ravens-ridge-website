Chicken Shogayaki

The ultimate Japanese "soul food"—savory, sharp, and fast.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced into very thin strips
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp honey
  • 1.5 tbsp freshly grated ginger
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • Shredded raw cabbage and cherry tomatoes for serving

    • Instructions

    1. Mix the soy sauce, sake, mirin, honey, and grated ginger in a bowl. Toss the chicken strips in the mixture and let sit for 10 minutes.
    2. Heat neutral oil in a large skillet over high heat. Remove chicken from marinade (save the liquid!) and sear the strips in a single layer until golden (about 2 mins).
    3. Pour the remaining marinade into the pan. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until the sauce reduces into a thick, glossy glaze that coats the chicken.
    4. Drizzle with sesame oil and serve immediately next to a pile of crunchy shredded cabbage.

    Recipe Notes

    • In a pinch, I substitute cooking sherry for sake. It's too expensive here for me to cook with it regularly.
    • I prefer chicken but you can make this with pork or beef as well.
    • I often will also serve this with jasmine rice and steamed veggies.
