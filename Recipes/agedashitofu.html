Agedashi Tofu (Crispy Silken Tofu)

A delicate, sophisticated dish featuring crispy tofu in a warm dashi broth.

Ingredients

  • 1 block firm or extra-firm Silken tofu
  • 1/2 cup potato starch (or cornstarch)
  • 1 cup dashi
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • Garnish: Grated ginger, grated daikon radish, and sliced scallions

    • Instructions

    1. Wrap the tofu in paper towels and place a weight on top for 15 minutes to remove excess moisture. Cut into large cubes.
    2. Combine dashi, soy sauce, and mirin in a small pot. Bring to a simmer and keep warm.
    3. Dredge each tofu cube in potato starch until fully covered. Shake off the excess.
    4. Heat 1-2 inches of neutral oil to 350°F (175°C). Fry the tofu for 2-3 minutes until the coating is crisp and translucent (it won't turn very brown).
    5. Place tofu in a shallow bowl and pour the warm broth around the base. Top with a dollop of ginger, daikon, and scallions. Drink the broth as you eat the tofu.

    Recipe Notes

    • Most times we eat this without the dashi sauce as it can be very sodium heavy.
