Ginger Chicken Tagine

A Moroccan-inspired slow-cooker style meal made in a standard pot.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb skinless chicken breast or trimmed thighs, cut into large chunks
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 2 tbsp grated ginger
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 lemon (zest and juice)
  • 1 cup chicken broth

    • Instructions

    1. In a heavy pot (Dutch oven), sauté the onion, ginger, and garlic in a little oil until soft.
    2. Add the turmeric, cumin, and cinnamon. Stir for 1 minute until the kitchen smells like a Moroccan market.
    3. Add the chicken, olives, lemon zest, juice, and broth.
    4. Place a drop lid (like in Nikujaga), then put the pan lid on.
    5. Cook on low for 25-30 minutes. The chicken will be incredibly tender and infused with the bright lemon and earthy spices.

    Recipe Notes

    • This is traditionally made with chicken thighs but I prefer the leaner meat of breasts.
    • This goes great with couscous and sweet potatoes.
