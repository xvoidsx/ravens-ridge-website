Citrus Ginger Steamed Fish

Bright and refreshing. Perfect for a light dinner.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Sea Bass or Catfish (fillets or a small whole fish)
  • 2-inch piece of fresh ginger (peeled and cut into matchsticks)
  • 3 scallions (whites and greens separated, julienned)
  • 1 small lemon (sliced into thin rounds)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp honey
  • 1 tbsp water (or Shaoxing wine/dry sherry)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil (like vegetable or peanut)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

Instructions

  1. Set up a steamer basket over boiling water. If you don't have one, you can place a heatproof plate on top of a small bowl inside a large pot with an inch of water.
  2. Pat the fish dry. Place half of your ginger matchsticks and lemon slices on the plate, set the fish on top, and scatter the remaining ginger and lemon slices over the fish.
  3. Place the plate in the steamer. Cover and steam for 8–10 minutes (for fillets) or until the fish is opaque and flakes easily with a chopstick.
  4. Carefully remove the plate (discard any excess liquid that pooled on the plate if you prefer a cleaner taste). Scatter the julienned scallions over the cooked fish.
  5. Mix soy sauce, honey, water (or Shaoxing wine/dry sherry), and lemon juice.
  6. In a small pan, heat the neutral oil and sesame oil until it just starts to shimmer or smoke slightly.
  7. Immediately pour the hot oil directly over the scallions and ginger—it should sizzle loudly. Finally, pour your sauce around the base of the fish rather than over the top to keep the skin/flesh from getting soggy.

Recipe Notes

  • Catfish fillets can be thicker than bass. If they are over an inch thick, give them an extra 2 minutes in the steamer.
  • I typically serve this with jasmine rice or a sweet potato and steamed veggies.
