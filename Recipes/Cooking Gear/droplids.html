Japanese Drop Lid (Otoshibuta)

When we started venturing into cooking more authentic Japanese foods, we learned about drop lids. It is used in several of our recipes like Nikujaga and Salmon Miso-ni.

What is Otoshibuta?

Otoshibuta, the Japanese Drop Lid, is essential for simmered foods that have delicate pieces you don't want to break down or want to enrich the flavor of. The drop lid prevents the ingredients from bouncing around too much and also allows the seasoning and broth, or sauce, to be evenly distributed, enhancing the flavor. When we started using the drop lids, it really changed the flavor profile for a lot of our recipes.

You can purchase wooden or stainless steel drop lids online or make your own at home with parchment paper or foil, which is what we do.

  • Stainless Steel Drop Lid- this is an adjustable otoshibuta made with stainless steel and most likely what we will purchase at some point.
  • Wooden Drop Lid- a traditional ototshibuta made from wood. The downfall with this is that you have to have a different size for each pot you need to use.
  • Make Your Own- this is what we do for now as is super simple, utilizing things we already have in the kitchen, and doesn't add yet another gadget to clutter an already small space.

How To Use Drop Lids

This is super simple, you simply place the drop lid in your pot directly over the ingredients. Typically, you won't use a regular lid with the drop lid unless you want to prevent the liquids from evaporating.
