Dragon's Breath Garlic Shrimp

The name comes from the "breath of the wok" (wok hei) and the punch of garlic and chili.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Large Shrimp (peeled and deveined, but leave the tails on for flavor)
  • 6–8 cloves of garlic (minced very fine)
  • 1 small red chili (sliced, or a pinch of red pepper flakes)
  • 1 tbsp minced fresh ginger
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • Squeeze of fresh orange juice (I use the juice of one orange)
  • A handful of fresh cilantro and a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds for garnish

    • Instructions

    1. Dry the Shrimp: This is the most important step. Use paper towels to get the shrimp as dry as possible. If they are wet, they will steam instead of searing.
    2. Heat 2 tbsp of neutral oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat until it’s shimmering. Add the shrimp in a single layer. Let them sit undisturbed for 1 minute to get a golden crust.
    3. Toss the shrimp, then clear a small space in the center of the pan. Drop in the garlic, ginger, and chili. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until the garlic turns golden (but don't let it burn!).
    4. Pour in the wine and orange juice. It will hiss and steam (that’s the "breath"). Toss everything together for another 30–60 seconds until the liquid reduces into a light, fragrant coating.
    5. Remove from heat immediately, drizzle with sesame oil, and top with cilantro and sesame seeds.

    Recipe Notes

    • I serve this with brown jasmine rice and fresh steamed veggies, like broccoli.
