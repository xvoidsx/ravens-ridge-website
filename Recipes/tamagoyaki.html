Tamagoyaki

My version of the traditional rolled egg omlete that is slightly savory with a hint of sweetness.

Ingredients

  • 3 Large Eggs
  • 1 tbsp Water or Dashi (Dashi adds that "umami" depth)
  • 1 tsp Honey (or sugar)
  • 1/2 tsp Soy Sauce
  • neutral oil for the pan

Instructions

  1. Whisk the eggs with the water (or dashi), honey, soy sauce, and salt. Crucial Tip: Use a cutting motion with your chopsticks or fork to break the yolks, but don't over-whisk. You want to avoid creating too many bubbles so the omelet stays dense and silky.
  2. Heat your pan over medium-low heat. Dip a folded paper towel in oil and wipe the pan so it’s lightly coated. Pour about 1/4 of the egg mixture into the pan, swirling it to cover the surface.
  3. As soon as the egg is set but still slightly "tacky" on top, use a spatula (or chopsticks) to fold the egg over itself from one side to the other. You’ll end up with a small, thin log at one end of the pan.
  4. Repeat (The "Bridge" Method)
    • Wipe the empty part of the pan with your oily paper towel again.
    • Push your little egg log to the side you just oiled.
    • Pour another 1/4 of the mixture into the empty space. Lift the existing egg log slightly so the new liquid flows underneath it (this "glues" the layers together)
    • When the new layer is set, roll the old log over the new layer to the other side.
  5. Repeat until all the egg is gone. Each time, your "log" will get thicker and more rectangular. Once finished, let it rest for a minute on a cutting board to firm up, then slice it into 1-inch thick "pillows."

Recipe Notes

  • If the egg sizzles loudly or browns instantly, your pan is too hot. Tamagoyaki should be a beautiful, consistent yellow.
  • If using a round pan, the ends of your log will be a bit messy. The secret is to just trim the messy ends off (and eat them as a "chef's snack") so the remaining slices look like perfect rectangles.
  • If you have a bamboo sushi mat, you can wrap the hot omelet in it for a minute to press it into a perfect shape and add those classic "ridges" on the outside.
