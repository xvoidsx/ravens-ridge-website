Vietnamese Shrimp Summer Rolls

Translucent, garden-fresh rolls that are the ultimate "clean" meal.

Ingredients

  • Large rice paper rounds (Spring roll wrappers)
  • 10-12 medium shrimp (poached and halved lengthwise)
  • fresh mint
  • cilantro
  • thin cucumber batons
  • lettuce leaves
  • Ginger-Lime Dip:
    • 2 tbsp lime juice(lemon works too)
    • 1 tsp honey
    • 1 tsp grated ginger
    • a pinch of red chili flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Whisk the lime, honey, ginger, and chili together. Set aside.
  2. Dip one rice paper round into warm water for 5 seconds until pliable. Lay it flat on a damp board.
  3. Place 3 shrimp halves in a row (pink side down for beauty). Layer lettuce, cucumber, and a generous amount of mint and cilantro on top.
  4. Fold the sides in and roll tightly like a burrito.
  5. Eat immediately while the paper is soft, dipping into the bright ginger-lime sauce.

Recipe Notes

  • It's not uncommon for the rice paper to sometimes tear on me. I just make sure that the tears get tucked in the rolls.
  • I did not include amounts on the greens because everyone will have a different taste. I find that even I alter the amounts depending on the flavor I want.
  • These are best when eaten fresh but I also will meal prep them and keep them in bentos in the fridge.
